### EXACT Inner Content:
```
AIDA Model (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)

The AIDA model is a classic marketing framework that outlines the 
cognitive stages a consumer goes through during the purchasing process:

1. Attention: Capturing the consumer's awareness
2. Interest: Engaging them with relevant information
3. Desire: Creating a want or need for the product/service
4. Action: Encouraging them to make a purchase

This model helps marketers design campaigns that guide consumers 
through each stage effectively.
```

### Flow Correlation:
- **Relationship**: Provides theoretical framework for journey stages

## NODE 2: Customer Journey Stages Definition (Green Box)
**Node Title**: [No explicit title - Journey definition box]
**Node Description**: Defines the five stages of customer journey with detailed explanations
**Node Position**: Top right of AIDA box, green background
**Node Type**: Journey Stage Definitions

### EXACT Inner Content:
```
Customer Journey Stages

1. Awareness: The customer becomes aware of a problem or need and 
   begins searching for solutions. They may discover your brand through 
   various channels.

2. Consideration: The customer evaluates different options and compares 
   solutions. They engage with content, reviews, and information to 
   make an informed decision.

3. Intent: The customer shows clear buying signals and intent to purchase. 
   They may add items to cart, request quotes, or engage with sales.

4. Conversion: The customer completes the purchase or desired action. 
   This is the transaction point.

5. Retention/Advocacy: Post-purchase engagement focused on satisfaction, 
   loyalty, and turning customers into brand advocates.
```

### Flow Correlation:
- **Connection**: Blue arrow pointing to Awareness stage workflow
- **Purpose**: Defines all stages with Awareness as entry point
- **Data Flow**: Stage definitions → Awareness implementation
